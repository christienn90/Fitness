Macro Targets by Day Type
Restaurant Strategy
🍽 The Restaurant Protocol

You'll eat out 1–2x per week. The plan accounts for this — restaurant days are treated as maintenance or slight surplus days, compensated by slightly tighter eating the day before and after. Don't skip a meal beforehand — you'll overeat.

Before the Restaurant
  • Eat a protein-rich meal 2–3 hours before (e.g. Greek yogurt + chicken) — blunts hunger, reduces overeating by 20–30%
  • Don't restrict calories all day to "save them" — this backfires and leads to bingeing
  • Drink 500ml water 30 min before — reduces appetite
At the Restaurant
  • Protein first strategy: Order the highest-protein main you can find — grilled fish, steak, chicken. Fill up on this before touching bread/fries
  • Avoid liquid calories: Alcohol, juices, sugary cocktails can add 300–600 kcal invisibly. Sparkling water, Diet Coke, black coffee
  • Skip the bread basket or limit to 1 piece max
  • Sauce on the side — restaurant sauces are calorie bombs, often 200–400 kcal per serving
  • Eyeball portions: Palm = ~150g protein, fist = ~200g carbs, thumb = ~15g fat
  • If it's Thai food (likely in Bangkok): choose stir-fried with minimal oil, Tom Yum soup, grilled proteins. Avoid coconut-heavy curries for the main
Day After a Restaurant
  • Don't punish yourself — just return to your normal plan
  • Optionally add a 10-min walk after meals to improve glucose disposal
  • Your next day's calories go back to the normal target for that day type
  • Weight may spike 1–2 kg the next morning — this is water/sodium retention, not fat. Ignore it
Success Principles
🏋️ Training

Keep lifting heavy. The biggest mistake during a cut is switching to high-rep light weight. Your body only keeps muscle it thinks it needs — heavy compound lifts (bench, squat, deadlift, rows) send the retention signal. Aim to maintain your current working weights throughout the cut, even if reps drop slightly.

🥩 Nutrition

Protein is non-negotiable at 190g/day. If you hit nothing else, hit protein. It has the highest thermic effect of food (~30% of calories burned in digestion), keeps you full the longest, and is the primary driver of muscle retention. Spread it across 4–5 meals, never go more than 4–5 hours without a protein source.

😴 Recovery

Sleep is a fat loss tool. Studies consistently show that cutting sleep from 8 to 5.5 hours shifts fat loss to muscle loss — same calorie deficit, worse body composition outcome. In a deficit your cortisol is already elevated. Protect sleep above everything else. 7.5–9 hours minimum.

🚶 NEAT

Non-exercise activity matters more than cardio. The treadmill sessions are great, but your total daily steps matter more consistently. Aim for 8,000–10,000 steps/day. In Bangkok this is easy — take the BTS, walk between places. This can add 200–400 kcal/day of extra burn without affecting recovery.

📊 Weigh-In Protocol

Weigh yourself every morning after toilet, before eating or drinking. Daily weights fluctuate 1–2 kg from water, sodium, glycogen. Use a 7-day rolling average to see the real trend. Don't react to single-day numbers. If your weekly average is dropping 0.3–0.5 kg, you're on track.

💧 Hydration

Drink 3–4 litres of water per day, especially in Bangkok heat. Dehydration impairs performance, increases hunger signals (thirst is often mistaken for hunger), and reduces fat oxidation efficiency. Start each morning with 500ml water before coffee.

⚠️ Warning Signs

Stop and reassess if: You lose more than 0.7 kg/week consistently (muscle loss likely), you feel chronically exhausted or can't complete workouts, libido drops significantly (testosterone signal), or strength drops more than 10% on main lifts. These mean your deficit is too aggressive — eat 200 kcal more per day.

☕ Appetite Management

Use these to manage hunger: Black coffee (suppresses appetite for 2–3 hours, avoid after 2pm), sparkling water (fills stomach), high-volume low-calorie foods (cucumber, celery, lettuce, shirataki noodles), waiting 20 minutes before a second serving (fullness signals lag behind eating). Chewing gum between meals also helps.

📅 Diet Breaks

Consider one maintenance day per week (eat at TDEE ~3,100 kcal). This refills muscle glycogen (improving gym performance), temporarily restores leptin (the satiety hormone that drops during deficits), and is psychologically sustainable. Your weekly average deficit stays the same. This is especially useful if you're going to a restaurant anyway — make that your high day.

🧠 Mindset

3.5 kg of fat is a small, focused goal. You're not doing an extreme cut — you're doing a refined one. At 16% with your muscle base, you have the best possible starting point. The risk isn't getting to 12%, it's rushing it and losing the muscle that makes 12% look impressive. Pace beats intensity here.

Weekly Treadmill Protocol
Incline Walk — 12° / 5–6 km/h / 30–45 min
Why this works: Incline walking at this intensity keeps you in Zone 2 (fat-burning zone, ~60–70% max HR) without elevating cortisol like running does. At 12° and 5.5 km/h you're burning roughly 420–500 kcal/session depending on bodyweight.

When to do it: Best done after lifting (glycogen depleted, higher fat oxidation) or in the morning fasted (optional — only if you don't feel weak). Never do hard cardio on leg day — it extends recovery time.

Keep 2–3 sessions/week max. More cardio → more cortisol → more muscle breakdown. The lifts are your primary tool.
